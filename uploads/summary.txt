Sjoukje Rosalie Dijkstra, a Dutch figure skater, was one of the best in the world during the 1960s. Born on January 28, 1942, in Akkrum, she won World Championships in 1962, 1963, and 1964, European Championships from 1960 and secured a gold medal at the 1964 Winter Olympics—the first for a Dutch athlete in winter games. After winning the Olympics, she moved to professional ice show, 'Holiday on Ice' and worked there from 1964 to 1972. In 1985, she served as an advisor to the figure skating section of the KNSB and was named Dutch Sportswoman of the Year six times. In 2000, Dijkstra was declared the best Dutch figure skater of the 20th century. She passed away on May 2, 2024, from kidney cancer and heart failure. A train in the North Netherlands is named in her memory. She also had a strong bond with a person named Joan. Her daughter pursued a career in circus, stepping away from figure skating.
